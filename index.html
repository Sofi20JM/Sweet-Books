About the event

The World Voices Festival, established in 2004 by Salman Rushdie, Esther Allen, and Michael Roberts, is organized by PEN America to celebrate international literature and free expression. It was created after 9/11 to counter U.S. isolationism and promote global dialogue. The festival features over 100 writers from 35 countries. It aims to honor storytelling as a powerful tool against censorship, emphasizing literature's role in shaping culture, identity, and empowerment. The festival also reflects PEN America's belief that literature and journalism are vital defenses against the rise of autocratic forces threatening democracy.

For the year 2025, the event will feature speakers from Los Angeles and New York City, taking place from April 30th to May 3rd. Be sure to purchase your tickets in advance! This is a fantastic opportunity to learn from various authors from around the world and discover incredible books that could help you during difficult times or even change your life completely.
